The Searchers (Ford, 1956)


ΛOWMOVIE point ="8.8"


The film tells the story of a racist war veteran who sets out to search for his nephew who was kidnapped by Indians, and how the purpose that motivates him becomes increasingly controversial during this search that lasts years.Although the film did not make a splash the year it was shot, it gained prestige over the years and became a classic. According to some, it is John Ford's masterpiece.'The Searchers' was selected among the 'culturally, historically and aesthetically important' films in America and it was decided to be preserved in the 'National Film Archive' of the Library of Congress.








Man with a Movie Camera (Vertov, 1927)


ΛOWMOVIE point ="7.4"


Soviet director and theorist Dziga Vertov's film depicts the USSR at a crucial transition point – a portrait of a Soviet city from sunrise to sunset. Vertov's enthusiastic and sometimes uplifting comments on the relationship between cinema, reality and history were groundbreaking. “Man with a Movie Camera” is a wordless tribute to the modern cities of the last century...











La Regle Du Jeu (Renoir, 1939)


ΛOWMOVIE point ="6.1"


Jean Renoir's masterpiece deals with the French aristocracy before World War II through the French bourgeoisie who come together on a hunting party. The events between the guests, their secret relationships, their views on the world and love are revealed. The film is also the last film the director shot in France before going to Hollywood. The film, which caused a stir and was censored at the time, appeared before the audience years later with the director's cut.
