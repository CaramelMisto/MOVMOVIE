A Beautiful Mind (Akiva Goldsman, 2002)


ΛOWMOVIE point ="7.0"


Nash begins to have dreams since his student years. After graduation, he gradually develops paranoid schizophrenia; but he cannot realize that he is sick. The chain of events changes when a psychiatrist suddenly appears before him during a conference. He is hospitalized and therefore moves away from his academic studies. When his illness reaches a point where he could harm his own child, his wife thinks he should go to the hospital again. Even though he cannot accept that he is sick for a long time, he realizes that the girl he sees all the time has never grown up. This makes him accept his illness. Nash will try to live with his imaginary realities by ignoring them. Even though the treatments he receives are not effective, he starts all over again with the support of his wife and former colleagues. He restrains his mental illness with his own mind and accelerates his academic studies again. He starts teaching at the university again. In the end, he continues his life with schizophrenia with his extraordinary struggle. And history presents this great genius with the Nobel Prize in Economics for overcoming his mental illness with his mind, devoting the rest of his life to science, and for the discoveries he made before the onset of his illness.








Big Fish (John August, 2004)


ΛOWMOVIE point ="5.4"


William returns home because his father is on his deathbed due to a terminal illness. In order to get to know his father better, he begins to collect stories about the youth of the man with a colorful personality. His father's experiences when he was a traveling salesman in his youth will fall into place like pieces of a puzzle, revealing the life of the incomprehensible man in its most fairy-tale aspect, with his victories and weaknesses.
