Inception (Christopher Nolan, 2010)


ΛOWMOVIE point ="6.3"


Dom Cobb is a very skilled thief. His specialty is extracting and stealing valuable secrets from deep within the subconscious during the dreaming moment, when the mind is most vulnerable. Cobb's rare prowess has made him a sought-after player in the dangerous new world of corporate espionage. However, it also made him an international fugitive and caused him to lose everything he loved. Cobb is offered an opportunity to escape his situation. One last job that could give him his life back; That is, if he can complete the impossible start... Instead of the perfect heist, Cobb and his team of professionals have to pull off the opposite this time; Their job is not to steal an idea but to plant it. If they succeed, it will be the perfect crime. But neither careful planning nor expertise can prepare them against dangerous enemies who seem to predict their every move. This is an enemy that only Cobb could see coming.








Avengers (Joss Whedon, Zak Penn , 2012)


ΛOWMOVIE point ="9.5"


The Avengers brings together Marvel's most iconic heroes: Iron Man, Hulk, Thor, Captain America, Hawkeye and Black Widow. An unexpected enemy surfaces and begins to threaten the security of the world. Nick Fury, director of the international peacekeeping agency known as SHIELD, realizes that he needs a team to save the world from such a disaster. He begins traveling around the world to select men for the team. Starring Robert Downey Jr., Chris Evans, Mark Ruffalo, Chris Hemsworth, Scarlett Johansson, Jeremy Renner and Tom Hiddleston, this team is accompanied by Stelan Skarsgard and Samuel L. Jackson. Written and directed by Joss Whedon from the story he wrote together with Zak Penn, The Avengers was adapted from "The Avengers", which was first published in 1963 and has been one of Marvel's most popular comic book series ever since. Get ready for a spectacular movie full of action and extraordinary special effects.











Sunrise (Murnau, 1927)


ΛOWMOVIE point ="5.9"


Murnau's film won the Oscar for best picture in 1928, and the famous star of the period, Janet Gaynor, was also deemed worthy of the Oscar for best actress for her performance in the film. In addition to Gaynor, the film also stars George O'Brien and Margaret Livingston. An urban woman, vacationing in the countryside, has an affair with a young, married local farmer and convinces the man to kill his wife and move to the big city with her. The man plans an 'accident' on the boat he and his wife will ride to the city, and hides a bunch of reeds in the boat to save himself from drowning. However, he does not have the heart to complete the plan and he rows and takes his wife to the shore. In great grief, they get on the tram and go to the city. Their tearful reconciliation ends in a church where a wedding is taking place. Having fallen in love with each other again, they visit first a photographer and then a barber. The man ignores the courtship of the manicurist at the barbershop and protects his wife from the attentions of a flirtatious admirer. After going to a huge amusement park and having fun, they return home by boat. However, a storm arises and the sail is torn.
