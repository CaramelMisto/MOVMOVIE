Vertigo (Hitchcock, 1958)


ΛOWMOVIE point ="7.4"


Vertigo is considered one of the most important works in the history of cinema in terms of Hitchcock's technique and subject matter. Detective Scottie Ferguson, who was unable to save his partner who fell off the roof while chasing a criminal, develops a fear of heights after this incident. He is a detective who quit his job and retired because of this fear that turned into "Vertigo" disease. He is hired by an old friend to watch his wife Madeleine, whose mental health he doubts. As Scottie watches the woman more closely, he realizes something is wrong; Moreover, he sees that the woman is suicidal. Now, following him from afar will not be enough to set things right, and will drag Scottie into a struggle where he will face his own fears.








2001: A Space Odyssey (Kubrick, 1968)


ΛOWMOVIE point ="4.4"


In 2001: A Space Odyssey, a group of primates are fighting for food in a deserted place. Their fight is interrupted by a mysterious black stone that suddenly appears next to them. This suddenly appearing stone has the power to enable primates to use tools for the first time. And thus, the use of reason, one of the most important steps of evolution, began and the dawn of humans came.
