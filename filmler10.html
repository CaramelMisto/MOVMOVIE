The Pianist ( Ronald Harwood,2002)


ΛOWMOVIE point ="8.7"


Wladyslaw Szpilman was 27 years old when the war broke out and was one of Poland's most promising concert pianists. He was playing Chopin's Nocturne in C minor when the Luftwaffe radio station was bombed. Like all Jews, he and his family were evicted from their homes and deported to the Warsaw ghettos. In his new life, this very talented young man started playing in bars where black marketeers and collaborators had fun. One of these collaborators saved him and his family from one of the prison camp trains that took them to death. Thanks to war whispers, resistance fighters and a surprise German officer, Szpilman manages to survive the war.
